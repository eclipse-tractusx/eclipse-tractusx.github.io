### Disclaimer

In the current Catena-X consortium, the various product teams have significantly shaped and defined the Catena-X data space including business use cases,
network services, and the architecture and have released tangible results such as standards, KITs and open-source reference implementations. The results are now to be handed over to the Catena-X association.

With the transition to an association, a new working model including (open) refinement and (open) plannings for PI11 must be implemented in order to further develop these results and to carry out the knowledge
transfer step by step by joint teams consisting of new association members and experienced consortium members under the governance of the Catena-X association.

The open planning days are the first step in this direction, but not all products are ready right now for an open planning. It might be the case, that some of the features are still defined within the jira, which is provided by the consortia.
In this case not all of the participants can access the features with jira.

_Please note_ In case there is a need to update an issue (e.g. adapt the description or link a subissue as a tasklist) committer rights are needed, or the initial creator of the issue has to do the changes.

These first alignment sessions will not be an introductory session - you should already be familiar with the source code of the service. We will introduce other formats to make sure you get onboarded and your voice is heard if you are new. We appreciate your understanding!
Our committers would highly appreciate contributions as a continuous stream of smaller, more manageable changes, rather than a big all-is-done chunk at the end of development. Thank you!

### Open Refinement Session on 19.10.2023

Join us for our **Open Refinement Session**, where we'll embark on a high-level exploration of our project's features and outstanding issues. This session offers a platform for developers, stakeholders, and enthusiasts
to come together and clarify project requirements in **dediacted breakoutsessions**. It's an opportunity to ask questions and ensure everyone has a shared understanding of the project's direction.

By participating, you'll not only contribute to a smoother development process but also help in shaping the project's roadmap.

**Be aware to refine your features within the sig-release repository ( on a high level until 17.10.2023 EOB ) and the related subtasks/issues in your product repository.**

Whether you're a core developer, a stakeholder, or someone passionate about our mission, your insights are vital to make informed decisions. Let's shape our vision into a structured, actionable plan together!
